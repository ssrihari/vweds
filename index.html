The previous day (Sunday, 22 January).

Come to the venue at 3pm, for the engagement ceremony. It's a formal, but regular before-the-wedding nischayadartham. Clothes will be given to the bride and groom as presents from the other side of parents, and they'll change into them. Friends and family will be there. And who knows, we might even exchange rings! :)

The ceremonies will end by 4pm, and we'll all have food. Then you can head back to your room, or hotel, and rest a while before the evening's celebrations. The bride and groom will probably do the same.

The evening's celebrations will start at 6:30pm! It's a semi-informal get-together of close friends and family. We'll spend most time doing the mingle mingle, walk talk, and most of all, take pictures! :D

At about 7:30, we'll have some more formal leg pulling, some toasts, a song or two, and a dance! Food will be around all evening, the small bites should be out earlier. And after dinner, we'll all do something nice, interesting, together, before we go to sleep :).

The day (Monday, 23 January).

The bride and groom will be up by 4. You can probably catch a little more sleep, and come to the venue at 7. And the script of the day unfolds. A Sriranga Yatra. The exchanging of garlands. The oonjal. The many attire changes. The solemn rituals in agni's sakshi. Mangalya dhaaranam. Saptapadi.
